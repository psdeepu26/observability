What's the difference between monitoring and observability?

Most teams set up dashboards and alerts — that's monitoring. It answers: "Is something broken?"
🩺 ELI5: Monitoring is like your car's "check engine" light — it tells you something is wrong. Observability is like having the full mechanic's diagnostic port — you can ask any question about what's happening inside, even problems nobody predicted.

📊 Observability (the real deal)

Observability is about being able to ask arbitrary questions of your system without having to know the questions in advance. The three pillars — metrics, logs, and traces — are the raw data, but observability is the capability those enable.

The key shift: monitoring tells you that there's a problem; observability tells you why. For something like NVCF where you're running serverless functions with cold starts, tail latencies, and concurrency limits, you can't pre-define every "check engine" light. You need to explore.

🔑 The one metric most teams get wrong

Percentiles, not averages. If your p99 latency is 200ms but your average is 20ms, your "average user" doesn't exist. For serverless platforms, always track p50, p95, and p99. Two users out of a hundred are having a terrible experience — averages hide them completely.
